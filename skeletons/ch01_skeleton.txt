The book launches when Jara sends Mila on Order mission through the portal to retrieve the blue stormfire gem (looks like a comet with a curvy tail). the mission starts at the Order ruins by the Great Tree (from book one). There's an operational portal gate that connects to the Order old travel network. The mission is a pretext. Jara is sending Mila to a "summer camp" to grow up a little.

Cold open:
A walk up to the gate tower: Mila whining, Jara deflecting, stormfire gem get mentioned as a mission goal; fox & cat in tow; the hedgehog reporting the gate's up and ready (his
inherited gate-vocation, as function not exposition); the bear snoozing in the shade.
Then:
Mila:"Do I have to? I don't want to go."
Fox:"I'll go!" … "What? We're treasure hunting. I'm not sitting this one out."
Jara:"It's Order business."
Fox:"Yeah, yeah—" — and steps through the portal himself, unbidden (selfpropelled; nobody sends the fox; he inserts himself because there's treasure).
Jara, dry:"Well. Hope he sticks the landing." (First hint the crossing is rough — a
"landing" you can fail — deadpan-ominous.)
Mila:"Wait — the landing?" (Her dread turns concrete one beat too late.)
Jara:"Have fun. Be careful." — and shoves her in (over the unanswered question;
the cheerful-armor + the ruthless-loving shove before the fear can take root).
HARD CUT — through with Mila. Strange place. She lands badly, starts to get up,
Wham — the cat flings through and knocks her flat. A cat-shaped projectile,
immediate, wordless, ungraceful. A tangle on the ground, strange world, no dignity.
Mila and the cat untangle on the floor of the gate-room. Through open doors: loud
footsteps, shouted commands, people running — they've dropped into the
middle of something (the city already tense/mobilized — withheld why;
established-by-overwhelm, no tour; they're behind from frame one).
A guard enters,freezes, then: "In here! There are more of them!" — "more of
them" pays the fox's head-start (he went through first, already spotted/chased
offscreen — the reader assembles "the fox is already out there stirring things up"
from one word). The trap snaps the instant they untangle (no grace period).
Mila, earnest, out of her depth: "We're here... on Order's business?"
CHAPTER END.
